POND PREPARATION AND MANAGEMENT FOR VANNAMEI SHRIMP FARMING
Source: Expert Aquaculture Guidelines, Indian Aquaculture Practices

=== POND PREPARATION BEFORE STOCKING ===

Step 1: Drain and Dry (10-15 days)
- Drain pond completely using pumps
- Expose bottom to sunlight for 10-15 days
- Sunlight destroys pathogens, parasites, and harmful organisms
- Till/plow pond bottom to expose all layers to sun

Step 2: Remove Sludge
- Remove accumulated sludge from bottom manually or using slurry pump
- Sludge contains harmful bacteria, ammonia-producing organic matter
- Focus on corners and center where sludge accumulates

Step 3: Lime Application
- Agricultural lime (CaCO3): 200-300 kg/acre for pH correction
- Burnt lime (CaO): 100-150 kg/acre for pathogen control (stronger)
- Dolomite: 200-300 kg/acre for both calcium and magnesium
- Spread evenly on dry pond bottom
- If previous disease: bleaching powder (chlorine) 30-50 kg/acre

Step 4: Tilling After Liming
- Till lime into soil to expose fresh bottom
- Let rest 3-5 days after lime application

Step 5: Water Filling
- Fill to 50-60% capacity initially
- Treat incoming water with chlorine (10-20 ppm) to kill wild organisms
- After 5-7 days, neutralize chlorine with sodium thiosulfate
- Then fill to final depth

Step 6: Water Treatment and Preparation
- Add EDTA (Ethylene Diamine Tetraacetic Acid): 2-3 kg/acre
  - Chelates heavy metals, makes water safer for PLs
- Add dolomite/lime to reach alkalinity 100-150 mg/L
- Fertilize to develop phytoplankton bloom:
  - Urea: 2-3 kg/acre
  - Single Super Phosphate (SSP): 1-2 kg/acre
  - Or use commercial plankton developer

Step 7: Phytoplankton Bloom Development (7-10 days)
- Wait for green/brown water color to develop
- Ideal Secchi depth: 30-40 cm
- DO should be 5+ mg/L by afternoon
- This natural bloom provides:
  - Oxygen during day
  - Natural food for shrimp
  - Stable pH buffer

Step 8: Stocking Check
- Measure all water parameters before stocking
- DO: above 5 mg/L
- pH: 7.8-8.3
- Temperature: 25-30°C
- Salinity: within 2 ppt of hatchery water

=== AERATION SETUP ===

Importance:
- Most critical factor after seed quality
- Underpowering aeration is leading cause of DO crash and mortality

Recommended aeration:
- 1-1.5 HP per 10,000 kg expected biomass (minimum)
- 2-3 HP per acre for semi-intensive (30-60 PL/m²)
- 4-6 HP per acre for intensive

Types:
- Paddlewheel aerators: most common in AP, create circulation and splash aeration
- Air blowers + diffusers: more energy efficient, subsurface aeration
- Emergency aerators: keep extra unit ready for power failure

Placement:
- Create circular water flow in pond
- No dead zones (corners covered)
- Center sludge accumulation (for easy removal)

Power backup:
- Must have generator for power cuts (especially in summer/monsoon)
- Power cut >2 hours can cause DO crash and mass mortality

=== PROBIOTICS PROGRAM ===

Probiotics are beneficial bacteria that:
- Break down organic waste (reducing ammonia and nitrite)
- Compete with harmful Vibrio bacteria
- Improve gut health of shrimp
- Stabilize water quality

Recommended schedule:
- Start: DOC 1 (day of stocking) — establish beneficial bacteria early
- Frequency: Every 7 days throughout crop
- Common products: Bacillus subtilis, B. licheniformis, Lactobacillus
- Application: Mix with water, broadcast in early morning when aerators running
- Dose: 1-2 kg/acre per application

Commercial probiotic brands (India):
- BioFish, Sanolife, Pond Plus, Microbe-Lift
- Many aqua-chemical companies also supply probiotics

=== WATER QUALITY CORRECTION CHEMICALS ===

Alkalinity low:
- Agricultural lime (CaCO3): 10-20 kg/acre raises alkalinity 10-15 mg/L
- Dolomite: same dose, also adds magnesium

pH too low:
- Quick lime (CaO): 5-10 kg/acre (mix with water first, apply in morning)
- Dolomite: 20-30 kg/acre (slower but safer)

pH too high:
- Partial water exchange
- Alum (Aluminum Sulfate): 2-5 kg/acre (use carefully)

Ammonia reduction:
- Stop or reduce feeding for 24-48 hours
- Probiotics + organic carbon (molasses)
- Water exchange 20-30%
- Zeolite: 20-30 kg/acre (absorbs ammonia temporarily)

Nitrite reduction:
- Salt (NaCl): 50-100 kg/acre (chloride ion competes with nitrite)
- Water exchange
- Probiotics

Turbid/muddy water:
- Agricultural gypsum (Calcium Sulfate): 20-30 kg/acre
- Settles suspended clay particles
- Improves water clarity

=== RECORD KEEPING BEST PRACTICES ===

Daily records to maintain:
- Date and DOC
- Morning DO and temperature
- pH (morning and afternoon)
- Feed quantity per feeding
- Feed tray response (hours to empty)
- Any abnormal observations
- Chemical/probiotic applications

Weekly records:
- Sampling data (ABW, count)
- Secchi depth
- Salinity and alkalinity
- Mortality count

This data helps:
- Calculate FCR (feed conversion ratio)
- Estimate harvest date and yield
- Identify problems early
- Improve future crops

=== BIOSECURITY PROTOCOL ===

Pond entry:
- Disinfect boots/footwear in lime water before entering pond area
- Wash hands with soap before handling shrimp or water

Equipment:
- Never share nets, buckets, aerator parts between ponds
- Disinfect shared equipment with bleach solution before use

Visitors:
- Restrict non-essential visitors during crop
- Vendors/buyers should not enter pond area

Water sources:
- Screen all water inlets with 60-80 mesh nets
- Never take water from ponds with disease outbreak nearby

Wild animals:
- Install bird netting (birds spread disease, especially WSSV)
- Control crabs and wild fish entering ponds (WSSV carriers)
- Seal gaps in pond bunds
