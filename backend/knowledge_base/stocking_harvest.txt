STOCKING AND HARVEST MANAGEMENT FOR VANNAMEI SHRIMP
Source: FAO Aquaculture, Expert Guidelines

=== STOCKING DENSITY RECOMMENDATIONS ===

By farming system:
- Extensive: 4-10 PL/m² (40,000-100,000 per acre)
- Semi-intensive: 10-30 PL/m² (100,000-300,000 per acre)
- Intensive: 60-150 PL/m² (600,000-1,500,000 per acre)
- Super-intensive: 300-450 PL/m² (with biofloc and indoor systems)

Standard for Indian inland farms (Andhra Pradesh):
- 30-60 PL/m² (300,000-600,000 per acre) most common
- 40-50 PL/m² (400,000-500,000) is balanced for 1-2 acre ponds

Higher stocking requires:
- More aeration (2-3 HP per 1,000 kg biomass minimum)
- More frequent water exchange
- Better feed management
- Higher biosecurity

=== PL (POST LARVAE) SELECTION CRITERIA ===

PL size at stocking: PL10-PL15 recommended (10-15 days post-metamorphosis)
- PL10: Body length ~7-8mm
- PL12: Body length ~8-10mm
- PL15: Body length ~10-12mm

Quality signs:
- Active, swimming against current when bucket stirred
- Uniform size within batch
- Full gut (dark line visible)
- No deformities
- PCR negative for WSSV, AHPND, EHP (demand test report)
- Stress test: >90% survive fresh water exposure for 5 minutes

Acclimation before stocking:
- Temperature: Match hatchery bag water to pond water (within 2°C)
- Salinity: Acclimatize gradually (1-2 ppt per hour)
- Float bags in pond for 20-30 minutes to equalize temperature
- Stock in early morning (5-7 AM) to avoid heat stress

=== GROWTH TARGETS ===

Average Body Weight (ABW) by DOC:
- DOC 15: 0.1-0.2g
- DOC 30: 1.0-2.0g
- DOC 45: 5.0-8.0g
- DOC 60: 10-14g
- DOC 75: 16-20g
- DOC 90: 20-25g
- DOC 100-110: 25-30g (harvest size for count 30-40)

Growth rate: 1.5-2.0g per week is good (intensive farming)

Size grades for market (India):
- Count 20: ~50g each (premium)
- Count 30: ~33g each (good price)
- Count 40: ~25g each (standard)
- Count 50: ~20g each (common)
- Count 60+: smaller size, lower price

=== SAMPLING PROTOCOL (EVERY 7-10 DAYS) ===

Equipment needed: Cast net, weighing scale, measuring tape

Steps:
1. Cast net at 3-5 locations around pond
2. Collect 50-100 shrimp sample
3. Weigh total sample on scale
4. ABW = Total weight ÷ Number of shrimp
5. Record DOC, ABW, any observations

Survival rate estimation:
- Biomass sampling method: difficult but more accurate
- Aerial count: track mortalities collected daily
- Feed conversion method: estimate based on feed consumed

=== HARVEST TIMING ===

Factors to consider:
1. Market price: Check daily price before deciding to harvest
2. Target size: Most profitable is count 30-40 (25-33g)
3. DOC: Usually harvest at DOC 90-120 depending on growth
4. Water quality: Deteriorating quality → harvest sooner
5. Weather: Avoid harvesting during heavy rain or extreme cold
6. Disease: Emergency harvest if disease detected

Partial harvest (intermediate harvest):
- At DOC 60-70, harvest 30-40% of biomass
- Use large-mesh net to remove bigger shrimp
- Reduces pond density, improves growth of remaining shrimp
- Good when market price is favorable

Full harvest:
- Drain pond 70-80% by night
- Harvest remaining shrimp using nets and pump
- Use ice (1:1 ratio ice to shrimp) immediately
- Process within 4 hours of harvest

=== PRODUCTION BENCHMARKS ===

Per acre per crop:
- Extensive: 150-500 kg
- Semi-intensive: 500-2,000 kg
- Intensive (Indian standard): 2,000-5,000 kg
- High-intensity: 5,000-10,000 kg

Crops per year:
- Most Indian farmers: 2 crops per year
- With good management: 3 crops possible (shorter DOC target)

=== PROFITABILITY CALCULATION ===

Income = Harvest weight (kg) × Market price (₹/kg)

Cost = Seed cost + Feed cost + Labour + Electricity + Chemicals + Pond rent

Feed cost is largest expense (typically 50-60% of total cost)
Seed cost: typically 10-15% of total cost

Break-even analysis example (1 acre, semi-intensive):
- Seeds: 200,000 PLs × ₹0.80 = ₹16,000
- Feed: 2,000 kg × ₹65/kg = ₹1,30,000
- Labour, electricity, chemicals: ₹50,000
- Total cost: ~₹2,00,000
- Revenue at count 40, ₹180/kg, 1500 kg harvest: ₹2,70,000
- Profit: ~₹70,000 per acre per crop

=== CROP PLANNING CALENDAR (ANDHRA PRADESH) ===

Crop 1:
- Stocking: February-March
- Harvest: June-July (DOC 90-120)
- Avoid monsoon stocking (July-August) due to disease risk

Crop 2:
- Stocking: August-September (after monsoon settles)
- Harvest: November-December
- Winter harvest often gets better price (festival season)

Between crops:
- Pond rest: 15-30 days minimum
- Pond preparation, drying, liming before next stock
