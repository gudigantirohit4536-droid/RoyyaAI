FEEDING MANAGEMENT FOR VANNAMEI SHRIMP (Penaeus vannamei)
Source: FAO Aquaculture, Expert Aquaculture Guidelines

=== PROTEIN REQUIREMENTS ===

Vannamei requires lower protein than other penaeid shrimp:
- Starter feeds (DOC 1-15): 40% protein
- Grower feeds (DOC 16-45): 35% protein
- Finisher feeds (DOC 46-harvest): 30-32% protein
This makes it more economical than tiger prawn farming.

=== FEEDING FREQUENCY BY DOC ===

DOC 1-10 (Post-stocking):
- Feed 4-5 times per day
- Small quantities — shrimp are tiny (0.01-0.05g)
- Use fine powdered or crumble starter feed
- No feed trays needed — broadcast across pond

DOC 11-30 (Early grow-out):
- Feed 4 times per day (6 AM, 10 AM, 2 PM, 6 PM)
- Begin using feed trays (anco trays) from DOC 15
- Adjust quantity based on tray response

DOC 31-60 (Mid grow-out):
- Feed 3-4 times per day
- Feed trays become primary adjustment method
- Increase feed 10% if trays are empty within 2 hours

DOC 61-harvest (Late grow-out):
- Feed 3 times per day (morning, afternoon, evening)
- Monitor ABW (average body weight) every 7-10 days
- Adjust feed based on biomass calculation

=== FEED QUANTITY CALCULATION ===

Formula: Daily Feed = Estimated Biomass × Feeding Rate %

Estimated Biomass = (Initial PL count × Survival Rate %) × Current ABW (kg)

Feeding Rate by DOC:
- DOC 1-15: 10-15% of body weight per day
- DOC 16-30: 7-10% per day
- DOC 31-45: 5-7% per day
- DOC 46-60: 3-5% per day
- DOC 61+: 2-3% per day

Example (1 acre pond, 100,000 PLs stocked, DOC 45, ABW = 8g, 80% survival):
- Surviving shrimp = 100,000 × 80% = 80,000
- Biomass = 80,000 × 8g = 640 kg
- Daily feed = 640 × 5% = 32 kg/day
- Per feeding = 32 ÷ 4 = 8 kg per feeding

=== FEED TRAY (ANCO) MANAGEMENT ===

Setup:
- Use 4-6 feed trays per acre
- Place at 1-2 meters depth, in open water away from aerators
- Check trays 2 hours after each feeding

Interpretation:
- Empty tray in <1.5 hours: INCREASE feed by 10-15%
- Empty tray in 1.5-2 hours: MAINTAIN current feed quantity (ideal)
- Feed remaining in tray: DECREASE feed by 10-20%
- Feed tray ignored completely: STOP feeding, check health/water quality

Feed tray quantity:
- Each tray = 1% of daily feed amount
- Example: Daily feed = 30 kg → 300g per tray (6 trays × 300g each = 1.8 kg total in trays)

=== FEED CONVERSION RATIO (FCR) ===

Definition: FCR = Total Feed Used ÷ Total Harvest Weight

Target FCR:
- Excellent: below 1.3
- Good: 1.3 - 1.5
- Average: 1.5 - 1.8
- Poor: above 1.8 (investigate feeding waste, disease, poor seed quality)

Factors that increase FCR (make it worse):
- Overfeeding (uneaten feed)
- Poor seed quality
- Disease causing reduced appetite
- Low DO (shrimp eat less)
- High stocking density without adequate aeration

=== SURVIVAL RATE BENCHMARKS ===

Excellent: >85%
Good: 75-85%
Average: 65-75%
Poor: below 65% (investigate disease, water quality, seed quality)

=== FEEDING DO'S AND DON'TS ===

DO:
- Store feed in dry, cool place (avoid moisture)
- Check feed expiry date — use within 3 months of manufacture
- Reduce feed by 50% if DO drops below 4 mg/L
- Reduce feed during disease outbreak or stress
- Feed at consistent times every day

DON'T:
- Never feed when DO is below 3.5 mg/L
- Never increase feed more than 10% per day
- Don't feed during emergency aeration
- Don't use wet or moldy feed (causes disease)
- Don't skip feed tray checks

=== SIGNS OF GOOD FEEDING ===

- Full stomach/gut visible (dark line along body)
- Active swimming when feed hits water
- Feed trays empty within 2 hours
- Consistent weight gain in sampling (5-7% per week)
- FCR trending below 1.5

=== SIGNS OF FEEDING PROBLEMS ===

- Shrimp not responding to feed being thrown
- Feed trays full even after 3+ hours
- Shrimp with empty stomachs on sampling
- Poor weight gain despite normal feeding
Action: Check DO, check health, check water quality before adjusting feed
