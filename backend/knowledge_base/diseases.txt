SHRIMP DISEASE MANAGEMENT FOR VANNAMEI (Penaeus vannamei)
Source: FAO Aquaculture, Expert Guidelines

=== MAJOR VIRAL DISEASES ===

1. WHITE SPOT SYNDROME VIRUS (WSSV) — White Spot Disease
Symptoms:
- White spots (0.5-2.0 mm diameter) on shell and body
- Reduced or stopped feeding (check feed trays)
- Shrimp clustering at pond edges and surface
- Reddish discoloration of body
- Lethargy, slow movement
- Mass mortality: 100% within 3-10 days if untreated
Risk factors:
- Temperature drops below 25°C
- Wild crustaceans entering pond (crabs, wild shrimp)
- Stress from low DO, bad water quality
Prevention:
- Use SPF (Specific Pathogen Free) seed from certified hatchery
- Install bird netting
- Screen all water inlets (60-80 mesh screen)
- Avoid wild broodstock
- Maintain temperature above 25°C
Treatment: NO CURE. Drain and disinfect pond with chlorine (30 ppm).

2. TAURA SYNDROME VIRUS (TSV)
Symptoms:
- Soft shell
- Empty gut (no food in stomach)
- Reddish tail fans
- Swimming erratically at surface
- Mortality 5-95%
Prevention: Use TSV-resistant (SPR) certified seed.

3. INFECTIOUS HYPODERMAL AND HAEMATOPOIETIC NECROSIS VIRUS (IHHNV)
Symptoms:
- Runted/dwarfed shrimp (RDS - Runt Deformity Syndrome)
- Bent or deformed rostrum (nose)
- Irregular growth among shrimp in same pond
- Shrimp don't reach market size
Prevention: Use IHHNV-free certified seed.

4. ACUTE HEPATOPANCREATIC NECROSIS DISEASE (AHPND/EMS)
Also called: Early Mortality Syndrome
Symptoms:
- Mass mortality in first 30 days (DOC 1-30)
- Pale or atrophied hepatopancreas (liver)
- Empty stomach and midgut
- White or yellow hepatopancreas when pressed
- Sudden mortality with no prior signs
Cause: Vibrio parahaemolyticus bacteria with specific toxin plasmid
Prevention:
- High biosecurity at stocking
- Water treatment before stocking
- Monitor Vibrio levels in water
- Add probiotics from DOC 1

=== MAJOR BACTERIAL DISEASES ===

5. VIBRIOSIS (Vibrio harveyi, V. alginolyticus, V. parahaemolyticus)
Symptoms:
- Red discoloration especially on tail/appendages
- Luminescent/glowing shrimp at night (V. harveyi)
- Shell disease — brown/black lesions on shell
- Slow swimming, lethargy
- Hepatopancreas discoloration
Risk factors:
- Poor water quality, high organic load
- High stocking density
- Overfeeding
Prevention and Control:
- Probiotics (Bacillus subtilis, Lactobacillus): 1-2 kg/acre every 7-10 days
- Reduce feeding by 30-50%
- Water exchange 20-30%
- Maintain adequate DO
- Avoid antibiotics (resistance development, export markets reject antibiotic-treated shrimp)

=== PARASITIC AND FUNGAL CONDITIONS ===

6. MICROSPORIDIOSIS (Enterocytozoon hepatopenaei — EHP)
Symptoms:
- Slow growth, stunted shrimp
- White streaks in muscle (cotton-like appearance)
- High variation in size within same cohort
- Feed trays look full but shrimp seem small
Risk: Cannot be seen with naked eye
Prevention:
- PCR testing of seed before stocking
- Disinfect ponds thoroughly between crops
- Dry pond bottom completely between crops

7. BLACK GILL (Gill Fouling)
Symptoms:
- Black or brown discoloration of gills
- Shrimp near surface gasping
- Reduced growth
Causes: Excessive algae, organic matter, fungi on gills
Control:
- Improve water circulation
- Reduce organic load
- Water exchange
- Add aquatic probiotics

=== NUTRITIONAL DISORDERS ===

8. BLUE BODY DISEASE
Symptoms:
- Shrimp turn blue/grey color
- Associated with white muscle
Cause: Nutritional deficiency (phosphorus, essential fatty acids)
Control: Feed quality improvement, fresh feed

=== POND PREPARATION (DISEASE PREVENTION) ===

After each crop — critical steps:
1. Drain pond completely
2. Dry pond bottom for 10-15 days minimum (sunlight kills pathogens)
3. Remove sludge from bottom (pump or physically remove)
4. Apply agricultural lime: 200-300 kg/acre (kills bacteria and pathogens)
5. If WSSV or AHPND outbreak: use bleaching powder 30 ppm + let pond sit 3-4 weeks
6. Fill water, treat with EDTA and chlorine before stocking

=== SEED QUALITY CHECKLIST (BEFORE BUYING) ===

- Buy only from certified SPF hatchery
- Check PCR test report: negative for WSSV, TSV, IHHNV, AHPND, EHP
- Visual check: uniform size, active swimming, no deformities
- Stress test: 30 shrimp in fresh water 5 minutes — survivors >90% needed
- Salinity test: acclimatize gradually, sudden change should have <10% mortality
- Do NOT buy cheapest PLs — seed quality is most important factor

=== DISEASE OUTBREAK RESPONSE PROTOCOL ===

If you see abnormal mortality (>0.5% per day):
1. Stop feeding immediately
2. Check all water parameters (DO, pH, temperature, salinity)
3. Collect samples — bring to lab or contact hatchery
4. Increase aeration
5. Do NOT do emergency water exchange (spreads disease to neighboring ponds)
6. Isolate equipment used in affected pond
7. Disinfect boots/hands before moving to other ponds
8. Contact local fisheries department or aquaculture extension officer

Normal mortality rates by DOC:
- DOC 1-15: up to 5% (post-stocking stress, adaptation)
- DOC 16-30: less than 0.2% per day
- DOC 31-60: less than 0.1% per day
- DOC 61+: less than 0.05% per day
